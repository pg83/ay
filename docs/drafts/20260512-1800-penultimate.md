## The three structural defects

### PR-A — `.global.a` carries the wrong `memberInputs` set

**File:** `/home/pg/monorepo/yatool/ar.go:347, :419, :438`
(`EmitARGlobal`, `EmitARGlobalNamedTagged`, `EmitARGlobalNamed`).

`emitARNode` is shared by both the plain `.a` and the `.global.a`
emit paths, and both receive the same `memberInputs` from the caller.
For a module like `devtools/ymake/libs/ymakeyaml`, REF produces:

- `.a` ⇒ 1493 inputs (full PEERDIR-header closure of yamake's .o set)
- `.global.a` ⇒ **3 inputs** (just the GLOBAL_SRCS .o + script)

OURS produces:

- `.a` ⇒ 797 (under-emit; see PR-B)
- `.global.a` ⇒ 1177 (over-emit; full closure leaks in)

The fix is in the caller of `EmitARGlobal*`: pass a `memberInputs`
set computed from GLOBAL_SRCS .o files only, not from the whole
module-wide member closure.

**Cascade-yield:** ~5300 input-lines, 9 nodes. Self-contained,
mechanical; no risk of regression elsewhere because `.global.a`
fingerprints currently fail and the fix narrows their inputs.

### PR-B — Header closure of .o set not propagated into AR/LD `inputs`

**Examples:** `libymake-lang-makelists.a` (REF 1493, OUR 797),
`liblibrary-cpp-build_info.a` (REF 806, OUR 8),
`liblibrary-cpp-packedtypes.a` (REF 953, OUR 822),
`libpy3python3-lib2-py.global.a` (REF 575, OUR 39),
`tools/struct2fieldcalc/struct2fieldcalc` LD (REF 1002, OUR 38).

In every case the .o list and the codegen-product handling are
correct (no over-emit on the .o side). The deficit is **only
SOURCE_ROOT headers** — almost entirely `contrib/libs/cxxsupp/libcxx`
(libcxx STL headers transitively included by the .o set),
`contrib/libs/musl`, `contrib/libs/glibcasm`,
`contrib/restricted/abseil-cpp[-tstring]`, and `contrib/libs/protobuf`.

REF rolls up the **union of header-transitive-closures of every member
.o** into the AR/LD `inputs`. OURS apparently rolls up the member .o
list correctly but the per-.o scanner's header set is dropped (or
filtered too aggressively) at the AR/LD aggregator. Where the modules
have *no* PEERDIRs touching libcxx, the AR is paired; the failures
cluster precisely at modules whose .o set reaches libcxx/musl/abseil/
protobuf headers.

Hypothesis: the closure walker is not following PEERDIR transitively
into header-only `.a` modules (libcxx, musl) when assembling
`memberInputs` for an AR — or it is following them but a stale filter
strips `contrib/libs/cxxsupp/...` entries on the way back up. The bias
is uniform; this is one bug, not five.

**Cascade-yield:** ~3700 input-lines, 14 AR nodes + 8 LD nodes.
Highest-leverage single fix. Risk: medium — it touches the closure
walker; a careless fix can over-attribute libcxx headers to nodes
that currently match (and regress L2 on the 95% paired population).

### PR-C — LD does not pull peer `.global.a` into `inputs`

**File:** caller of `EmitLD` in the LD path.

`ymake/bin/ymake` LD: 173 inputs in REF, 149 in OURS. All 24 missing
inputs are `*.global.a` or proto-`.a` paths that **already exist
elsewhere in OUR graph as outputs** — we generate the global archives
but do not declare them as LD inputs of the consuming binary. Same
pattern at `tools/py3cc/slow/py3cc` (+7), `tools/rescompiler` (+1),
`tools/rescompressor` (+1), `contrib/tools/protoc` (+1),
`contrib/tools/protoc/plugins/cpp_styleguide` (+1).

The LD's PEERDIR-closure walker is collecting `.a` archives but
skipping the matching `.global.a` (and a handful of proto `.a` like
`libymake-diag-common_msg.a`, `library/cpp/eventlog/proto/...`).

**Cascade-yield:** ~40 archive-references across 8 LD nodes, but the
graph-correctness yield is large — these `.global.a` are how global
init code propagates; missing them is a soundness defect, not just a
fingerprint defect.

## Top 3 PRs by cascade-yield

1. **PR-M3-AR-header-closure** (theme B). Fix the AR/LD `memberInputs`
   walker to include the full header transitive closure of every member
   .o, matching REF. **Lift: ~3700 input-lines across 22 nodes; closes
   the L2 deficit at every libcxx/musl/abseil/protobuf-consuming AR
   and the LD struct2fieldcalc case.** Risk: medium — touches shared
   walker code.
2. **PR-M3-globalA-narrow-closure** (theme A). Split the `memberInputs`
   feed in `emitARNode` so `.global.a` receives only the GLOBAL_SRCS-
   local closure. Lift: ~5300 over-emitted input-lines across 9
   nodes. Risk: low — surgical change to caller signatures; only
   `.global.a` outputs are affected.
3. **PR-M3-LD-peer-globalA** (theme C). Extend the LD PEERDIR-closure
   walker to include matching `.global.a` (and small proto-`.a`)
   outputs. Lift: ~40 archive refs across 8 LD binaries, plus
   correctness fix for global-init linkage. Risk: low–medium — LD
   walker change but with a clear acceptance criterion (the missing
   24 refs at `ymake/bin/ymake` reappear).

**Most cascade-yielding move: PR-M3-AR-header-closure (theme B).**
It is one fix that unlocks 22 nodes and 3700 input-lines, and it is
the same root cause that propagates into the LD-hub deficits at
struct2fieldcalc/py3cc. Doing B before A is correct ordering: B
*defines* what the full member-closure should be; A then *narrows*
the global-archive variant from that correctly-defined full set.

Order of execution: **B → A → C → D/E/F/G/H tail**.

After A+B+C the L2 mismatch count should drop from 73 to ≤10, the L3
mismatch count from 32 to ≤20 (D/F also nudge L3 via tail args), and

## 2. Linchpin PRs

Two PRs unblock the majority of remaining residue. They are the only ones
whose lift is *cascade-driven* rather than node-count-driven.

### Linchpin α — **PR-M3-peer-walk-order**: preserve direct-peer declaration order through composer/walker

**Why it is the linchpin.** It fixes all **260 order-only L3** nodes (83 %
of L3 mismatch by node-count) and a side-of-house benefit: the 7 obj/lib
reorder nodes (which are L3 *and* L2 contributors when the .o list is the
input list of an AR). The root cause is upstream of every CC node in the
graph — every TU's cmd_args contains a transitive PEERDIR-derived -I and -D
block, so an order regression in the walker fans out to hundreds of nodes.

**Where to fix.** Likely in `composers.go` / the walker glue that materialises
direct PEERDIR results before flattening. Probable site: a `sort.Slice` or
map iteration that loses source order. Goal: iterate direct peers in the
order the AST recorded them (PEERDIR declaration order in the ya.make).

**M1/M2 risk.** M1/M2 byte-exact must hold. The change is read-side
(graph composition / cmd materialisation), not data-side (ya.make/conf
ingestion). M1 (yamake.proto / textproto canon) and M2 (configure-graph
text dump) are produced by emitters that do **not** participate in the
peer-walk over CC translation units. Risk: **low**, but verify M1+M2
sha-equality post-change before declaring done.

### Linchpin β — **PR-M3-proto-runtime-closure**: complete the protobuf runtime preproc closure for `.pb.cc.o` and bind-reg3 TUs

**Why it is a linchpin.** It fixes simultaneously:

- L2: 14 `.pb.cc.o`/`.pb.cc.pic.o` + parent `.a` nodes, each missing ~477
  abseil-tstring + protobuf-src + abseil-cpp + libcxx headers — together
  ≈ 6,500 missing input edges. This is the dominant L2 edge cluster
  outside cxxsupp.
- L3: the 9+9+11+1 = 30 `-I` ref-only-arg findings for protobuf-src /
  abseil-cpp-tstring / abseil-cpp / single-protobuf in the same 14 + 11
  reg3 TUs.

PR-M3-F-7-D enriched `protobuf/runtime` `EmitsIncludes` partially; the
gap is that the **transitive header closure of those EmitsIncludes** is
not walked. When a `.pb.cc` includes `protobuf/runtime/google/protobuf/...`,
the scanner must walk into abseil-cpp-tstring (which protobuf headers
include) and bring its closure too.

**Where to fix.** `scanner.go` (transitive emit closure of EmitsIncludes
sets) or `gen.go` (the place where protobuf/runtime exports the
abseil-tstring closure to peers). One of these is missing a level of
walking.

**M1/M2 risk.** **Low.** M1 is the structural .proto/.textproto dump; M2
is the configure graph text dump. Neither contains preproc closures.

### PR-3 — **M3-proto-runtime-closure** (LINCHPIN β)

- **Scope:** Two coupled fixes:
  1. When a TU includes a `protobuf/runtime` `EmitsIncludes` header, walk
     the full transitive include closure (abseil-cpp-tstring → libcxx) of
     that header into the TU's `inputs` field.
  2. When `protobuf/runtime` is a transitive peer, emit
     `-I.../protobuf/src`, `-I.../abseil-cpp`, `-I.../abseil-cpp-tstring`
     into the TU's cmd_args, not only for `.pb.cc.o` but also for any TU

- **Expected lift:**
  - L2: 14 `.pb.cc.o` / `.pb.cc.pic.o` / their `.a` containers + 4 retry +

**PR-1 (PR-M3-peer-walk-order) first, alone, and ship it.** It closes 260
